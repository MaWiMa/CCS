== Chapter 9

=== Leading the Way: The Role of School Administrators in Integration

_by Darlene E. Perner_

A new era for integrating exceptional students began in August 1986 when
an innovative education law came into effect in New Brunswick, Canada. It
was introduced as Bill 85. Part of the legislation rquires the integration
of exceptional students. As stated in the Working Guidelines on Integration:

[quote, New Brunswick Department of Education, 1988]
____
School boards one instructed to place exceptional pupils in regular classrooms
with non-exceptional pupils unless such placement proves detrimental to
the needs of the child or other children. Whereas formerly a case had to
be made to include an exceptional pupil in a regular class, this section
of the legislation requires that a case must be made to remove a child from
a regular class.
____
